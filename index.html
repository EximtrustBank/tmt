📜 History of Turkmen Manat

The Turkmen manat was introduced on November 1, 1993, replacing the Soviet and Russian ruble at a rate of 500 RUR = 1 TMM. The manat consists of 100 tenge. The ISO 4217 code is TMT (previously TMM). On January 1, 2009, a new manat was introduced with a denomination of 5000 old manats = 1 new manat (TMT). The currency is issued by the Central Bank of Turkmenistan. In 2025, polymer banknotes of 1, 5, 10, and a new 200 manat note were issued, dedicated to the 30th anniversary of Turkmenistan's neutrality. The official dollar exchange rate has been artificially set at 3.5 TMT per USD since 2015, but the black market rate is more than 10 times higher (around 40 TMT per USD as of 2021).
